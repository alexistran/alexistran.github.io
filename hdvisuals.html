HD Visuals

High definition visuals to help people better envision their dream spaces.


Winter 2021

UX Designer: research, ideation, interaction and interface design, user flows, prototyping, usability testing, UI documentation

The Problem

Designers are always looking for ways to improve the materials they’re providing to their clients. The Capture Mode feature in our room planner application allows designers to capture visuals for their customers, but some designers are still having trouble conveying their vision. The rendered imagery helps customers understand the space better, but it’s not perfect and there still exists a gap between the image render and what it would look like in real life.

The Solution

Photo-realistic imagery that lets customers really picture themselves in their newly designed spaces.

Create HD Images

Take standard renders to the next level by making them photo-realistic.

Updated Photo Boards

A living and breathing collection of all the photos you captured. This is where new photo-realistic renders can be created, and all the images can used to create deliverables for customers.

The Process


Defining Requirements

Taking into account what we wanted to create, I worked closely with the Engineering team to define requirements for this feature to exist within our timeline. R&D engineers worked towards developing higher quality renders and Design (me!) worked towards smoothly integrating this functionality into our existing application.

  1. HD images will be generated based off existing standard quality image captures.
  2. HD images use more resources than our existing standard quality capture images and take more time to generate (a few mins). We want to limit users to only be able to generate 1 HD image at a time while we work towards improving our processes and resources.
  3. Users need to be notified and kept informed during the process because there is a bit of delay.

Initial Design & Feedback



Initially we almost went with throwing the option into the existing capture modal. However after testing on a few users and discussion with the cross functional team, the way HD images is capable of functioning was more easily understood to be more of a “filter” applied after to an existing standard quality image, rather than captured simultaneously with the standard quality image. We also didn't want to completely remove standard quality images in favor of high quality ones because the render time for HD images is still quite long.

With this information, the entry point of HD image creation was moved to the Photo Boards.

Informing the User

With the HD images still having limitations (such as taking as long as 15 mins to generate), we needed to keep the user informed throughout the process. We made sure to incorporate several key notifications:

  • In Progress (to inform user that their render has started)
  • HD Render Complete (to inform user that their render is ready)

Using the HD Images

Once the image is ready, designers can go to their Photo Boards to view and sort their visuals and create collages from these images for their customers.

Next Steps

  • While this is a huge improvement over how we originally began, there are always things we could do to take it to the next level. Usability testing and understanding how this new feature plays a role in a designer’s flow would be logical next steps. After, we’d want to explore other ways that we can make the collateral generated from designer to customer more succinct and helpful. The ultimate goal of this app is to be an all-in-one tool for the designer’s entire process from initial design to hand off.

Takeaways

  • It’s critical to keep users informed.
    Especially critical when there can be longer wait times/delays.
  • Cross functional alignment is key to a smooth handoff.
    It was incredibly helpful to have the engineers always on the same page with regular discussions so that there wasn’t (as much) confusion at the actual handoff.
